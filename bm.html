BIRSA MUNDA

The Fearless Tribal Revolutionary Who Challenged the British Colonial Rulers

LIFE OF BIRSA MUNDA

  • November 15, 1875: Birsa Munda is born in Ulihatu, Bihar (now in Jharkhand), India.
  • 1886: Birsa is sent to a German Missionary school at Burju where he learns about Christianity.
  • 1890: Birsa and his family convert to Christianity and join the German Mission at Burju.
  • 1891: Birsa's family is evicted from their ancestral land by British authorities and moneylenders.
  • 1893: Birsa participates in the anti-British and anti-missionary movement led by the Munda tribe.
  • 1894: Birsa starts gaining followers and becomes a prominent leader among the Mundas.
  • 1895: Birsa declares himself a prophet and begins propagating the faith of Birsait, which becomes very popular among the Munda tribals.
  • 1897: Birsa is arrested for participating in the Sardari Larai movement, which was against the British governmentâ€™s policies.
  • 1898: Birsa is released from jail and continues to lead his movement against the British and the missionaries.
  • 1899-1900: Birsa leads the Munda Rebellion (Ulgulan) against British colonial rule, fighting for the rights of the tribal community. The rebellion includes several guerrilla warfare tactics and attacks on police stations and British officials.
  • January 1900: Birsa Munda is arrested by British forces while he is asleep in Jamkopai forest, Chakradharpur.
  • June 9, 1900: Birsa Munda dies in British custody under mysterious circumstances in the Ranchi jail.

Posthumous Recognition:

Birsa Munda is remembered as a great tribal freedom fighter, and his life and struggle are celebrated in Jharkhand and across India. His birth anniversary is celebrated as Birsa Munda Jayanti, and Ranchi University is named after him. Monuments and memorials are established in his honor, and he is declared a national hero.
